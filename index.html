CE Labs

CE Labs aims to understand consciousness and how to engineer it. We design experiments to probe the mechanics and structure of experience and develop neurotechnologies to interface with it.

In the coming months, we will conduct a set of tFUS experiments:

Posterior cingulate cortex: Whether modulation shifts self-related experience, sense of time, or memory-linked effects.

Thalamus: Whether thalamic modulation shifts conscious access, arousal, or level of consciousness.

dACC and posterior insula: Whether these targets shift pain, valence, or unpleasantness in distinct ways.

NAcc and sgACC: Whether reward-related targets shift salience, reward, or motivational tone.

Pallidum and related subcortical targets: Whether subcortical arousal circuitry offers another route into arousal and access.

Psychedelic states: The same target tests under psychedelic conditions.

Disorders of consciousness: Whether arousal, access, or communication can be restored in low-output patients.

Our long term resolution is to be target- and modality-agnostic and to follow the approach that seems most promising. With a small number of experiments, we believe we can shed light on the central problems of consciousness and begin building neurotechnologies that give humans broad control over conscious experience.
